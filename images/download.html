Sometimes a phone comes along that makes you do a double take not because it’s flashy, but because you cant quite believe everything it offers for the price. The Tecno Spark 40 Pro Plus, launched in July 2025, is exactly that kind of device.

With a 144Hz AMOLED, Android 15, Mediatek Helio G200, 30W wireless charging, and a premium camera setup all while maintaining its budget roots this phone makes a bold statement in the mid-tier space.

Lets get into it.

Design: Style With Substance

Though Tecno hasnt published the official weight and dimensions, whats clear is that the Spark 40 Pro Plus doesnt feel like a budget phone in the hand.

The back is plastic, but finished with a premium gloss, available in Nebula Black, Aurora White, Moon Titanium, and Tundra Green. These colors feel deliberate neither too flashy nor boring and help the phone stand out subtly.

The phone carries an IP64 rating, which means it’s dust-tight and resistant to water splashes a nice-to-have safety net for users in dusty or rainy environments.

One key design upgrade? The under-display optical fingerprint scanner a premium touch rarely seen in this range.

Display: Arguably the Best at This Price

Lets talk numbers:

  • 6.78-inch AMOLED
  • 1B colors
  • 1220 x 2712 resolution
  • 144Hz refresh rate
  • 439 PPI
  • PWM dimming for eye comfort

For a phone in the Spark series, this is a jaw-dropper.

This is the kind of screen that feels flagship-grade at first glance. It’s sharp, incredibly fluid, and buttery smooth thanks to the 144Hz refresh rate. The high PPI makes text and images look crisp, and the AMOLED panel delivers true blacks and vivid colors.

Whether youre gaming, watching movies, or just scrolling social feeds, this screen makes it feel like youre using a much more expensive device.

The only downside? No Gorilla Glass or any confirmed protection. Youll want a good case and screen protector right out of the box.

Performance: Helio G200 Holds Its Own

Powering the Spark 40 Pro Plus is MediaTeks Helio G200, a 6nm octa-core chip (2x Cortex-A76 + 6x Cortex-A55) paired with Mali-G57 MC2 GPU running at 1.1GHz.

On paper, it may not compete with the Snapdragon 7 Gen series or Dimensity chips, but in actual use:

  • Apps open quickly.
  • Multitasking is snappy with 8GB RAM.
  • Day-to-day performance is smooth thanks to optimization on Android 15 with HiOS 15.

You can push it for gaming titles like PUBG Mobile, Mobile Legends, and Asphalt 9 run well at medium settings. Its not a performance beast, but its consistent, which is more important for most people.

You also get 128GB or 256GB of internal storage with microSDXC support, which keeps the phone flexible for large files or local media.

Battery: Fast, Wireless, and Even Reverse Charging

Heres where Tecno blows past nearly all its competition:

  • 5200 mAh battery
  • 45W wired charging
  • 30W wireless charging
  • 5W reverse wireless charging

Wireless charging at all is rare in mid-range Android phones. But 30W wireless? Thats basically flagship-tier.

This setup gives the Spark 40 Pro Plus some serious everyday convenience:

  • Full charge wired in just over an hour
  • Wireless charging pad on your desk? Youre set.
  • Need to charge a friends earbuds or phone? Use reverse wireless in a pinch.

Battery life itself is solid around 6.5 to 7.5 hours of screen time consistently, even with 144Hz on. And HiOS 15’s battery optimizations help stretch standby time further than youd expect.

Camera: Simple Setup, Surprisingly Good Results

Rear Camera:

  • 50 MP main (wide), PDAF
  • Auxiliary sensor (likely depth or AI lens)

You get:

  • 1440p video at 30fps
  • Dual LED flash
  • HDR

In good lighting, this camera does very well crisp shots, decent detail retention, and natural colors. The AI scene detection and HDR help balance out tricky lighting. Night shots benefit from the strong dual-LED flash but do struggle slightly in dynamic range.

Front Camera:

  • 13 MP wide sensor
  • Dual-LED dual-tone flash
  • 1080p@30fps video

Selfies are solid in all conditions. The front flash really helps with indoor or low-light video calls and vlogging. Its a rare inclusion that makes a big difference in practice.

Overall, this isnt a photography powerhouse, but it punches above its weight especially when considering the price bracket.

Audio & Extras: Polished and Packed

The Spark 40 Pro Plus includes:

  • Stereo speakers loud and clear, with decent balance
  • 3.5mm headphone jack
  • Hi-Res audio + Hi-Res Wireless audio (24-bit/192kHz)
  • FM Radio (rare in 2025!)
  • Infrared blaster (great for remote control apps)

This combination makes it a true media phone. Whether wired or wireless, audio quality is noticeably richer than competitors in this range.

Connectivity

You get:

  • Dual-SIM (Nano + Nano)
  • 4G LTE with decent band support
  • Wi-Fi 802.11 a/b/g/n/ac (dual-band)
  • Bluetooth 5.3
  • USB-C 2.0 with OTG
  • NFC
  • GPS & Infrared

No 5G, but everything else is here including NFC, which is often missing in mid-range phones. For digital payments, wireless audio, and file transfers, this phone is fully equipped.

Verdict: The Unexpected Flagship Killer for Budget Buyers

The Tecno Spark 40 Pro Plus might just be the most feature-rich mid-range phone under the radar in 2025. It breaks expectations in all the right ways:

  • AMOLED 144Hz display with stunning visuals
  • Flagship-class charging with wireless & reverse wireless
  • Hi-Res audio, stereo speakers, 3.5mm jack
  • Polished camera setup for day-to-day photography
  • Smooth, modern Android 15 experience with HiOS 15

And it does all this while keeping things affordable and accessible.
